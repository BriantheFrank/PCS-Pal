How to Use This Tool

Follow the guide in order. Start with the big picture of the move and work down into the details you control day by day.

1. Move Inventory (Move Organizer)

Purpose: Build a complete household inventory that matches how movers pack and how claims are reviewed.

  1. Create one entry for each room, including garages and storage.
  2. Do a first pass for large, high-value items only.
  3. Flag high-value or sentimental items before pack-out.
  4. Add boxed items by room as boxes are packed.
  5. Label boxes with the room name and box number to match the list.
  6. Verify every room and high-value item is captured before pack-out.

Tip: Keep labels short and consistent (e.g., “Kitchen – Pots & Pans”) so movers can match boxes quickly.

2. Family Itinerary & Calendar

Purpose: Create a shared timeline of when and where your family is moving.

  1. Set your departure location and date.
  2. Add each overnight stop in order, with lodging details if you have them.
  3. Enter your final destination and arrival date.
  4. Review the auto-filled calendar and treat it as the source of truth.
  5. Update itinerary entries as plans change so the calendar stays accurate.

Tip: Share the calendar early so everyone plans around the same dates.

3. Packers & Move Logistics

Purpose: Track dates and access windows that determine when movers can work.

  1. Enter the day or date range the packers will be on site.
  2. Check for conflicts with travel days or school events.
  3. Use the calendar to coordinate arrival, inspections, and handoffs.

Tip: Keep the calendar handy during calls so everyone references the same schedule.

4. Destination Base Information

Purpose: Centralize trusted details about your gaining installation so you can plan the first week.

  1. Review the base overview for location and key facilities.
  2. Use the base map to identify gates, housing, medical, and schools.
  3. Plan first-week priorities like lodging, check-in, and appointments.

Tip: Save the most important addresses before travel day.

5. Custom Events & Notes

Purpose: Capture anything unique to your PCS move that doesn’t fit elsewhere.

  1. Add custom events such as appointments, vehicle drop-off, or school enrollment.
  2. Store notes and contacts like phone numbers and reminders.
  3. Review the calendar to confirm every custom event appears alongside the itinerary.

Tip: Add contacts the moment you receive them so nothing is lost.
